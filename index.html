Background

About

I study the aerodynamics of rotating bodies at high speeds — a problem that sits at the intersection of viscous flow theory, experimental measurement, and numerical simulation. My doctoral thesis at IISc focuses on the unsteady flow field around spinning conical geometries, with implications for the accurate prediction of forces and moments on spinning projectiles, re-entry vehicles, and similar aerospace configurations.

My experimental work employs stereo and phase-locked Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV) for three-component velocity field measurements, supplemented by Schlieren imaging and oil-flow visualization in supersonic wind tunnels (Mach 2) at the National Aerospace Laboratories (NAL), Bengaluru. On the computational side, I develop structured and unstructured meshes and perform steady and transient RANS simulations using ANSYS Fluent, integrating analytical 1D flow models with 3D CFD results to build physically consistent interpretations of experimental data.

Before joining IISc, I completed an M.Tech in Cryogenic Engineering at IIT Kharagpur and a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering at NIT Manipur, which gave me a strong foundation in thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and engineering design.

Focus Areas

Research

🌀

Rotating Body Aerodynamics

Investigating the unsteady viscous flow field around spinning conical and cone-cylinder bodies, with a focus on lift-up phenomena, flow instability, and Magnus-type effects.

  • Spinning cone/cone-cylinder experiments in enclosed wind tunnel sections
  • Identification of viscous lift-up and flow instability mechanisms
  • Encoder-based phase-locked measurement systems (Arduino + IR sensors)
🔬

Experimental Flow Diagnostics

Applying optical and flow visualization methods to capture three-dimensional, time-resolved velocity and density fields in complex aerodynamic configurations.

  • Stereo and phase-locked PIV for 3-component velocity fields
  • Dual-pulse Nd:YAG laser and high-speed CCD/CMOS camera systems
  • Schlieren imaging and oil-flow visualization
💨

High-Speed & Compressible Flows

Studying aerodynamics in supersonic and transonic regimes relevant to projectile and re-entry vehicle design, using both experimental wind-tunnel facilities and CFD.

  • Supersonic tunnel experiments at Mach 2 (NAL, Bengaluru)
  • Steady and transient RANS simulations (ANSYS Fluent)
  • Structured/unstructured mesh generation (ICEM CFD)
💻

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Developing and applying CFD frameworks to simulate subsonic and supersonic flows around rotating geometries, and integrating analytical models with 3D simulation results.

  • Integrated 1D analytical and transient 3D CFD interpretation
  • Subsonic and supersonic regime simulations
  • GPU-accelerated image processing pipelines for Schlieren data
📊

Data-Driven Flow Analysis

Developing computational tools that leverage machine learning and GPU acceleration to extract quantitative information from large experimental datasets.

  • Sub-pixel shock angle estimation from Schlieren sequences
  • GPU-accelerated shock tracking (flow-hpc-shocktrack)
  • Transformer-based LLMs for scientific document summarization
🛠

Instrumentation & Prototyping

Designing custom hardware and synchronization electronics for rotating-model experiments, including motor control systems and encoder-based timing setups.

  • BLDC motor control and high-speed rotating rigs
  • Arduino-based encoder synchronization for phase-locked measurements
  • CNC machining and CATIA-based model design

Pedagogy

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that engineering students learn best when fundamental theory is connected directly to physical observation. In fluid mechanics, a simple flow visualization — a streak of oil on a surface, a Schlieren shadowgraph of a shock wave — can make abstract concepts immediately tangible. I aim to cultivate this physical intuition alongside mathematical rigor. I am committed to making technical material accessible without sacrificing depth, and to building an environment where students feel comfortable asking questions and testing ideas. Laboratory sessions and hands-on problem-solving are central to how I teach.
